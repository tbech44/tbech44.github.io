Heinz Field

Heinz Field is the home of both the Pittsburgh Steelers and the Pitt Panthers football teams since its completion in 2001. It has a capacity of 68,400 with 129 executive suites. It cost $281 million to construct.

In 2011 the stadium hosted the NHL Winter Classic. It also occasionally hosts concerts and other events, and was the site of Pitt's recent victory over PSU. The attendance at that game was the highest in Pittsburgh sports history at 69,983 people.

Heinz Field is home to the Pittsburgh Steelers, but also the Pitt Panthers. It's dual-purpose nature makes it twice as important as most NFL stadiums.


















PNC Park

PNC Park opened in 2001, making it one of the newer stadiums in the MLB. It cost $216 million to construct and has a capacity of 38,362. It is the home of the Pittsburgh Pirates but it also occasionally hosts concerts and has appeared in several Hollywood films.
